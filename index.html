// A roadmap to launch your tech repair business — built by you.

Foundations

[1.1] Business Setup
  • Choose your structure: LLC, Sole Proprietor, or Corporation
  • Register your business name and domain (yourtechfix.com)
  • Set up tax ID (EIN) and a business bank account
  • Apply for local permits or repair certifications
[1.2] Equipment + Tools
  • Essential tools: soldering station, multimeter, anti-static mat
  • Specialized repair tools for phones, PCs, and consoles
  • Data recovery & backup drives
[1.3] Branding + Identity
  • Logo and glow-themed branding
  • Create social profiles + Google Business page
  • Tagline example: "Fast. Honest. Expert Tech Revival."

Studying / Learning / Tips

[2.1] Learning & Certifications
  • Enroll in CompTIA A+ or Apple Certified Technician programs
  • Follow YouTube repair educators (Louis Rossmann, iFixit, etc.)
  • Study diagnostic software and hardware schematics
[2.2] Business Tips
  • Build trust: transparent pricing, quick replies
  • Offer warranties (30–90 days)
  • Encourage Google reviews + social proof

Customer Systems

[3.1] CRM (Customer Relations Management)
  • Track repairs and customer history
  • Tools: HubSpot, Notion, or self-built database
  • Follow-up emails for customer retention
[3.2] NET (Networking & Contacts)
  • Connect with local schools / IT pros / small businesses
  • Form supplier relationships for parts
  • Tech meetups and repair community groups
  • Email template example: 
    [SUBJECT]: Partnership Inquiry  
Hello [Name],
I run a local tech repair service specializing in fast, reliable device repairs.
Would you be open to networking or referral collaboration?
- [Your Name]
